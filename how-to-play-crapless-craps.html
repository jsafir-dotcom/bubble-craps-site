How to Play Crapless Craps

Crapless craps is a variation of traditional craps where you don’t lose on 2, 3, or 12 on the come-out roll. Instead, those numbers become point numbers.

This makes the game more exciting—but also changes the odds.

Step 1: Place a Pass Line Bet

Like standard craps, the game starts with a pass line bet before the come-out roll.

Step 2: The Come-Out Roll

  • If the shooter rolls a 7 or 11 → you win
  • If the shooter rolls 2, 3, or 12 → in crapless craps, these become point numbers
  • Any other number becomes the point

Step 3: Point is Established

The point can be: 2, 3, 4, 5, 6, 8, 9, 10, 11, or 12

The goal is to roll that number again before a 7.

Step 4: Win or Lose

  • If the point is rolled again → you win
  • If a 7 is rolled first → you lose

Key Difference vs Standard Craps

In regular craps, 2, 3, and 12 lose immediately. In crapless craps, they stay in play—but are very hard to hit again.

Why Crapless Craps Is Riskier

Numbers like 2 and 12 are extremely unlikely to roll again before a 7. This increases the house edge compared to standard craps.
